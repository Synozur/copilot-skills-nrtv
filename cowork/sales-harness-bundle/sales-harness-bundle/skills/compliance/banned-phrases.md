# Banned Phrases

Any of these in a draft → fail, redraft. The Composer must not emit these.

## Outbound clichés

- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "I hope this finds you well"
- "Hope you are doing well"
- "I came across your profile"
- "I came across your LinkedIn"
- "I noticed we are both"
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "I wanted to introduce myself"
- "Just touching base"
- "Touching base"
- "Circling back"
- "Quick question"
- "Quick favor"
- "Sorry to bother you"
- "Apologies for the cold outreach"

## Hype / vague-value language

- "Game changer"
- "Game-changer"
- "Best-in-class"
- "World-class"
- "Industry-leading"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Synergy"
- "Synergies"
- "Leverage" (as a verb in marketing sense)
- "Empower"
- "Empowering"
- "Revolutionary"
- "Transformative"
- "Unlock value"
- "Move the needle"

## Subject-line bans

- Any subject starting with "Quick"
- Any subject ending in "?" longer than 4 words
- Any subject with "RE:" prepended manually
- "Following up"
- "Touching base"
- "Following Up" (any casing)

## False-familiarity bans

- "I know you must be busy"
- "I'm sure you get a lot of these"
- "I'll be brief" (and then not being brief)

## Voice-specific bans

These are merged in from `outbound-voice/voice-dna.md` at extraction time. The Composer should treat the union of this file and the voice-dna banned-phrases section as the active blocklist.
